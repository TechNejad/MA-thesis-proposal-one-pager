Playing the Part: How Role Specifications Shape LLM Agent Interactions
Work-in-progress master's thesis proposal
This thesis investigates how Role Specifications—structured framings of persona, goals, norms, tactics, time beliefs, and contextual pressures—shape LLM agent interactions in mixed-motive resource sharing. While existing studies use role prompts as background scaffolding, this work makes them the unified independent variable, exploring role-based framing as a possible soft-alignment mechanism for agent interactions.
Main Research Question
How do Role Specifications influence cooperation, communication, and adaptation dynamics among LLM agents in repeated social dilemmas, and where do they fall short?
Simulation Design
An Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma where two city agents negotiate monthly water allocations during a drought, choosing to cooperate (fair split) or defect (over-draw). A pre-decision negotiation phase allows for dynamic interactions.
Summary of role specifications:
Sustainability Officer
norm-enforcement, long-termism
City Growth Planner
demand-driven, short-term pressure
Water Security Commissioner
risk-averse, graduated sanctions
Full role components inspired by Ostrom's commons principles and Axelrod's strategy archetypes.
Data & Analysis
Full negotiation transcripts tagged for persuasion, defection, and role adherence/drift · Payoff metrics tracking cooperation rates · Post-simulation agent interviews · Qualitative Comparative Analysis (QCA) across role pairings to identify conditions for trust and conflict.
Theoretical Grounding
Game Theory — Provides the structural foundation through Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma dynamics, payoff structures, and the "shadow of the future"
Dialogue Theory — Analyzes how agents use "cheap talk," commitments, and persuasion tactics
Symbolic Interactionism — Frames roles as performed and dynamic, enabling analysis of role adherence versus drift
Commons Governance — Ostrom's design principles inspire role component specifications
Evolutionary Game Theory — Axelrod's strategy archetypes inform role behavioral patterns
AI Alignment — Examines role-prompting as a soft-control strategy
Expected Contribution
Introduces Role Specifications as a testable independent variable in multi-agent LLM research · Provides empirical evidence on how role framing affects cooperation, persuasion, and adaptation · Offers practical guidance for designing prosocial, interpretable agent societies
Deliverables: Thesis paper · Open-source code · Policy briefs · Academic posters · Role-spec templates · Dataset & transcripts
