Simulated Norms: The Fragile Business of Prompt Engineering Ethical Norms in Multi-Agent LLM Systems
Work-in-progress master's thesis
The Central Problem
Normative role design for LLMs is happening through rapid, unsystematic, and haphazard prompt programming. Developers, researchers, and systems designers are embedding ethical assumptions into agents' roles through trial-and-error, often focusing on outcomes rather than ethical coherence.
Why it Matters
  1. AI agents are being deployed in real-world systems: energy grids, supply chains, healthcare, and education.
  2. Our design choices, how we encode ethical norms, directly influence their decisions and societal impact.
  3. Scale matters: These systems operate at massive levels, affecting millions of users and critical infrastructures.
Main Research Question
How are ethical norms encoded into agents across different applications of Multi-Agent Systems, and what are their socio technical implications?
Research Approach
Design: Structured Thematic Analysis

Data: Research papers (methods, claims, evaluation techniques) and their code repositories (system prompts and agent role specifications).

Process:
  1. Initial Coding: Code papers based on key dimensions.
  2. Theme Formation: Cluster codes into overarching themes.
  3. In-Depth Analysis: Analyze each theme using individual papers as concrete examples.
  4. Synthesis: Identify connections and tensions to answer the research question.
Theoretical Framework
Three normative ethical theories guide the analysis:
Deontological
rules, duties, obligations
Consequentialist
outcomes, utility, optimization
Virtue Ethics
character traits, social roles
Based on Woodgate & Ajmeri (2024), Macro Ethics Principles for Responsible AI Systems
Analytical Framework
Papers will be systematically coded across multiple dimensions:
Ethical Framework Classification — Which of the three ethical theories (deontological, consequentialist, virtue ethics) are invoked in agent design?
Normative Mechanisms — What types of mechanisms (fairness, reciprocity, rights, duties) are embedded in agent roles?
Evaluation Strategies — How do researchers assess normative behavior in their experiments?
Domain-Specific Patterns — How does the simulation domain (negotiation, resource allocation, governance) influence normative design choices?
Gaps and Tensions — What inconsistencies exist between researchers' claims and their methods?
These dimensions provide initial structure, but the framework remains open to emergent patterns and additional criteria discovered during iterative coding.
Expected Contribution
Typology of normative design patterns · Evidence-based guidelines for encoding ethics · Risk identification for AI governance · Practical insights for developers and policymakers
Deliverables: Thesis · Briefs · Conference papers · Workshops · Design taxonomy
